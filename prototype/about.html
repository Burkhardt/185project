RNA Folder: harnessing game data to predict RNA secondary structures

The browser game RNA Folder investigates the possibility of harnessing user data to predict RNA secondary structures. Users can fold short RNA molecules with known secondary structures with the goal of creating formations very similar to the known structures. The user develops a sense for what good formations look like, and then applies this newly acquired intuition to fold RNAs with unknown structures.

User data is collected and analyzed and solutions that are submitted by many different users over and over are classified as promising candidates.
